# Small Business Digital Economics Research

**Date:** March 2026
**Purpose:** Understand the real economics of how small/local businesses spend money on digital services, to inform Surgent's pricing, positioning, and value proposition.

---

## 1. Total Digital Spend by Industry

### Dental Practices

- **Revenue range:** $500K-$2M/year for a typical solo/small practice
- **Marketing as % of revenue:** 2-7% for established practices; up to 20% for new practices
- **Monthly marketing spend:** $1,000-$5,000+/month
  - 54% of dentists spend $1,000+/month on marketing
  - 22% spend over $5,000/month
- **Patient acquisition cost:** $150-$500 per new patient
  - Google Ads (PPC): $150-$350/patient
  - SEO/Organic: $50-$150/patient
  - Referrals: $25-$75/patient
  - Social media ads: $200-$400/patient
  - Direct mail: $300-$600/patient
- **Patient lifetime value:** $500-$800/year for general dentistry
- **Target LTV:CAC ratio:** Minimum 3:1

### Law Firms (Solo/Small)

- **Marketing as % of revenue:** 10-15% for solo, 7-12% for small firms
- **Monthly marketing spend:**
  - Solo practitioners: $1,000-$3,000/month
  - Small firms (2-10 attorneys): $3,000-$10,000/month
  - High-growth firms allocate ~16.5% of revenue to marketing vs. 5% for no-growth firms
- **Cost per lead:** $649 blended ($784 paid, $516 organic)
- **Customer acquisition cost:** $749 average ($584 organic, $1,245 inorganic)
- **Trend:** 30% of firms increasing budgets for 2026; costs skyrocketing in PPC/SEO

### Home Services (Plumber, HVAC, Electrician)

- **Marketing as % of revenue:** 7-15% of revenue
- **Monthly digital spend ranges:**
  - Maintenance-level: $500-$1,500/month
  - Growth-focused: $1,500-$5,000/month
  - Aggressive growth: $5,000-$15,000+/month
- **Cost per lead:**
  - HVAC: $92 blended ($115 paid, $69 organic)
  - Plumbing: $55-$120
  - Google Local Service Ads: $25-$38/lead
- **Customer acquisition cost (HVAC):** $296 average ($211 organic, $549 inorganic)
- **Trend:** CPL increased for 69% of home services businesses, average +10.5% YoY

### Restaurants

- **Marketing as % of revenue:** 3-6% for established; 6-10% for new
- **Average monthly revenue:** ~$112K
- **Monthly marketing spend:** $3,300-$6,700/month (for average restaurant)
- **Small restaurants (<$5M revenue):** 7-8% of revenue

## 5. Price Sensitivity and Willingness to Pay

### Budget Distribution (What SMBs Actually Spend)

| Monthly Marketing Budget  | % of Small Businesses                       |
| ------------------------- | ------------------------------------------- |
| Under $500/month          | ~37% (55% of businesses with <10 employees) |
| Under $1,000/month        | **52%**                                     |
| $1,000-$10,000/year total | 19%                                         |
| Over $10,000/year total   | 15%                                         |

### Key Price Sensitivity Signals

- **50% have NO full-time marketing employee** -- they are doing it themselves
- **60% spend only 1-10 hours/week** on marketing
- **Price is the #1 factor** when selecting a marketing partner
- **74% say "one-stop shop" is important**, but only **38% say "very important"**
- **66% cite economic uncertainty** as top challenge (up from 48%)
- **Partnership with agencies dropped from 60% to 34%** in one year -- businesses are pulling back to DIY

### Pricing Tier Analysis for All-in-One Solution

**$29/month tier (Starter/Basic)**

- Targets the 52% spending under $1,000/month
- Competes with: Wix ($23-$39), Squarespace ($25-$36), Mailchimp ($13-$20)
- This is "replace your website builder + basic email" pricing
- Massive addressable market but low ARPU

**$79-$99/month tier (Growth)**

- Targets businesses spending $1,000-$5,000/month on marketing
- Competes with: website builder + CRM + email tool combo ($100-$300/month)
- Must replace 3-4 separate tools to justify price
- Sweet spot for solo dentists, plumbers, small restaurants

**$199-$297/month tier (Professional)**

- Targets businesses with $5,000+/month marketing budgets
- Competes with: GoHighLevel agency pricing ($197-$497/month)
- Must include ads management, advanced automation, AI features
- Targets practices doing $500K+ revenue

### Competitive Pricing Context

| Current Spend (Typical Tool Stack)                                             | Surgent Replacement Price | Savings        |
| ------------------------------------------------------------------------------ | ------------------------- | -------------- |
| Website ($35) + Email ($30) + CRM ($0-$45) = $65-$110                          | $29-$79/month             | 0-55% savings  |
| Website ($35) + Email ($50) + CRM ($45) + Social ($30) + Reviews ($300) = $460 | $99-$199/month            | 57-79% savings |

## 6. ROI Expectations

### What Small Businesses Expect

- **67% of small businesses** make under $3 for every $1 spent on ads
- **14%** achieve $3-$6 ROAS
- **Only 3%** achieve $6-$9 ROAS
- **General benchmark:** 3:1 ROI is considered "good" for SMBs
- **Email marketing:** $38 return per $1 spent (3,800% ROI -- highest channel)
- **Google Ads:** Average $2 return per $1 spent
- **SEO:** 5:1+ ROI long-term, but takes 3-6 months

### Channel ROI Rankings

1. **Email marketing** -- 380% ROI ($38 per $1)
2. **SEO/Content** -- 13x more ROI than non-blogging businesses (long-term)
3. **Search advertising** -- 2:1 average, higher satisfaction
4. **Social media** -- 70% brand awareness improvement, up to 50% sales lift
5. **Paid social** -- Lower direct ROAS but good for awareness

### Timeline Expectations

- **Paid ads:** Results within weeks
- **SEO/Content:** 3-6 months for measurable traction
- **Automation/Email:** 1-3 months to build lists and see returns

## 7. Subscription Fatigue and Consolidation Trends

### The Consolidation Wave

- **By 2027, 50%+ of enterprises** will use "super apps" to consolidate tech stacks
- **33% of organizations** consolidated apps in the last year
- **Agency usage dropped from 60% to 34%** among SMBs (2025 to 2026) -- massive shift to self-service
- **24% planning to cut traditional media** (up from 16%)
- SaaS expansion revenue growth decelerating as customers "scrutinize usage and consolidate tools"

### What This Means for Surgent

- Businesses are actively consolidating -- they WANT fewer tools
- They're dropping agencies in favor of DIY -- they need powerful but simple tools
- One-third of SaaS spend is wasted -- the "we replace 5 tools" message resonates
- Economic uncertainty (66% cite it) makes consolidation more attractive
- But "one-stop shop" is only "very important" to 38% -- the product must prove value per feature, not just bundle

### AI Adoption Accelerating the Shift

- **60% of small businesses** now use AI in marketing (stable YoY)
- **81% use AI for content creation** (up from 52%)
- **84% use AI to save time** (up from 61%)
- **78% use AI for ideation** (up from 50%)
- AI is making DIY marketing viable for business owners who previously needed agencies

## 8. Key Insights for Surgent Pricing & Positioning

### The Opportunity

1. **52% of small businesses spend under $1,000/month on ALL marketing** -- they need affordable tools
2. **50% have no marketing staff** -- they need AI-powered simplicity
3. **Agency usage collapsed from 60% to 34%** -- massive unserved market looking for self-service
4. **AI adoption in marketing is exploding** -- 81% for content, up from 52%
5. **Subscription fatigue is real** -- 33% consolidated tools last year

### The Right Price Points

- **$29-$39/month** captures the "replace my website builder" market (~$228/year ARPU matches Squarespace)
- **$79-$99/month** captures the "replace my tool stack" market (saves $300-500/month)
- **$199-$297/month** captures the "replace my agency" market (saves $700-$2,700/month)

### The Value Proposition Math

For a dentist spending $2,000/month across 5-6 tools + some agency help:

- Surgent at $99-$199/month = 90-95% savings
- If Surgent helps acquire even 2-3 new patients/month at $150-$300 CAC saved = $300-$900 additional value
- Total value delivery: $2,000+ in replaced costs and generated revenue

For a plumber spending $1,000/month on scattered marketing:

- Surgent at $79-$99/month = 90-92% savings
- If Surgent helps land 3-5 leads/month at $80-$120 CPL saved = $240-$600 additional value

### Critical Success Metrics to Target

- **ARPU goal:** $30-$50/month at scale (beats Squarespace/Wix at ~$19/month)
- **LTV:CAC ratio:** 3:1 minimum for Surgent's own customer acquisition
- **Churn reduction:** Bundle stickiness should outperform single-product tools
- **Expansion revenue:** Start at $29, upsell to $99-$199 as businesses grow
