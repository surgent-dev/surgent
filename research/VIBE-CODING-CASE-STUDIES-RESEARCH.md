## 1. Revenue-Generating Consumer Apps

### Fly (fly.pieter.com) — Flight Simulator Game

- **Builder:** Pieter Levels (indie maker / digital nomad, not a game developer)
- **Category:** Gaming / Entertainment
- **What it is:** Multiplayer browser-based 3D flight simulator with dogfights
- **Build time:** Initial prototype in 3 hours (Feb 2025)
- **Tools:** Cursor AI, Three.js, WebSockets, PeerJS, GPT-4, Grok 3, Claude (~90% AI-generated)
- **Revenue:** $0 to $1M ARR in 17 days. Reports range from $50K-$87K/month MRR
- **Users:** 320,000+ players
- **Notable:** Elon Musk retweeted saying "Wow, this is cool. AI gaming will be massive"
- **Sources:** [404 Media](https://www.404media.co/this-game-created-by-ai-vibe-coding-makes-50-000-a-month-yours-probably-wont/), [Indie Hackers](https://www.indiehackers.com/post/tech/pieter-levels-used-ai-to-build-a-viral-flight-simulator-in-3-hours-with-no-background-in-game-development-7CPfMr1yRLEwH6cC8xhE), [Medium](https://medium.com/coding-beauty/vibe-coding-1m-arr-17-days-079cd7fd707a)

## Key Patterns & Takeaways

### Who is building:

- **Non-technical founders** (growth marketers, HR professionals, designers, journalists, marketing execs) are building and shipping real products
- **Technical founders** are building 5-10x faster, shipping MVPs in hours instead of months
- **Teenagers** (Cal AI) are building $30M+ revenue businesses
- **Solo founders** (Base44) are building $80M exits in 6 months

### What they're building:

- **Games/entertainment** dominate the viral successes (Fly, Vibe Sail)
- **Micro-SaaS** is the most common monetization path (ChatIQ, TrendFeed, Illustration.app)
- **Consumer safety/health** apps (Plinq, Cal AI, Aphasio)
- **Internal enterprise tools** (Virgin Voyages, EvenUp)
- **EdTech** (Qconcursos, Mindarin)

### Revenue ranges:

- **Micro-SaaS side projects:** $1.7K-$12K/month
- **Successful consumer apps:** $50K-$87K/month (Fly)
- **Breakout hits:** $30-40M/year (Cal AI)
- **First-week launches:** $135K (SendPush), $3M in 48 hours (Qconcursos)
- **Exits:** $80M cash (Base44 to Wix after 6 months)

### Build timelines:

- **10 minutes:** Simple prototype (LunchBox Buddy)
- **3 hours:** Viral game prototype (Fly)
- **4 hours:** Finance dashboard (ETHval)
- **4 days:** SaaS MVP hitting $10K MRR (TrendFeed)
- **1 weekend:** Messaging app (Bitchat)
- **2 weeks:** Enterprise feature launch generating $3M (Qconcursos)
- **45 days:** Safety platform reaching $456K ARR (Plinq)
- **2 months:** App Store app (Dog-e-dex, Aphasio)

### Common tool stacks:

- **Cursor** + Claude/GPT-4 (most common for technical builders)
- **Lovable** (most common for non-technical builders)
- **Bolt.new** (quick prototypes)
- **Replit** (full-stack autonomous builds)
- **Claude Code** (command-line developers)

### Cautionary notes:

- **Security:** Jack Dorsey's Bitchat had a man-in-the-middle vulnerability discovered within 24 hours of launch
- **Adidas pilot:** First attempt with AI coding tools "spectacularly flopped" before succeeding on the second try
- **Code quality:** Multiple sources warn about "comprehension debt" — founders owning codebases they don't understand
- **Sustainability:** Many viral successes are ad-revenue dependent; long-term defensibility remains unproven
