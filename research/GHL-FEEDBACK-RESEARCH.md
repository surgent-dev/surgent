# GoHighLevel User Feedback & Feature Requests: Comprehensive Analysis

_Research date: March 29, 2026_

---

## Executive Summary

GoHighLevel (GHL) is an all-in-one marketing/CRM platform primarily serving agencies ($97-$497/mo). It has 600K+ users and a 4.6/5 on G2. Despite strong loyalty from agencies, there is a **highly polarized** user base with consistent, deep frustrations. The platform's core value proposition is **consolidation + white-label at aggressive pricing**, but individual features lose to best-in-class dedicated tools in every category.

**The fundamental tension:** GHL "does many things adequately; it does very few things excellently."

## 2. Top Pain Points (Ranked by Severity & Frequency)

### #1: Email Deliverability (THE biggest complaint)

- Uses Mailgun (branded "LC Email") with **shared IP reputation**
- Users migrating from ActiveCampaign/Mailchimp report open rates crashing from **35-40% down to 9%**
- No built-in email throttling -- users on custom SMTP get deliverability destroyed
- Requires manual SPF/DKIM/DMARC configuration that most users don't know how to do
- Per-send charges: $0.675/1,000 sends ($0.01/email). 30K subscriber list = **$300/month** on top of subscription
- Email builder produces "wonky spacing, weird fonts, and mediocre mobile scaling"
- Feature request titled "Emails look awful!" has 193 votes
- **Not suitable for cold outreach** without supplementary tools

### #2: Platform Performance (Extremely Slow)

- 154+ votes on ideas portal documenting persistent slowness
- Users report **60+ second page load times** and crashes
- "At times it is literally unusable"
- Desktop app "just as slow" as web version
- Persists even on gigabit internet with no browser extensions
- GHL-hosted websites score poorly on Google PageSpeed Insights
- Users "actively reviewing alternative CRMs due to performance"

### #3: Steep Learning Curve

- Functional competence: **2-3 weeks**; confident use: **6-8 weeks**
- One business owner took a **full year** to feel competent
- Settings scattered across disconnected menus -- "three separate settings panels"
- Help documents "often obsolete, incorrect, or missing altogether"
- UI looks "completely different from training guides"
- Most businesses only use **20-30% of features** but pay for everything
- Non-technical users find it overwhelming without implementation help

### #4: Hidden & Usage-Based Costs

| Cost             | Rate                |
| ---------------- | ------------------- |
| Email            | $0.675/1,000 sends  |
| SMS (US)         | $0.0079/segment     |
| SMS (Australia)  | $0.06-$0.09/message |
| Phone (inbound)  | $0.013/min          |
| Phone (outbound) | $0.026/min          |
| AI interactions  | $0.02-$0.07 each    |
| Phone numbers    | $1.15/mo each       |

- **Typical agency adds $70-$150/month** in usage fees
- Heavy users: $400-$600/month on a $297 plan
- One user's total cost **exceeded Keap's $249/month** despite GHL's $97/mo marketing
- One user charged **$600 to export their data**
- BBB complaint: **$14,403.14 in unauthorized charges**

### #5: Customer Support Quality (Deteriorating)

- "Support went from horrific, to good once you can talk to someone, then to terrible"
- Ticket response times: 24-48 hours (sometimes a week+)
- Complex issues get "circular responses"
- Some staff "admit they don't know how the system works"
- Support asks for **5-star Trustpilot reviews during support calls** while failing to resolve issues
- Priority support costs **$300/month extra**
- GHL does NOT support white-label end users -- resellers must handle all support

### #6: UI/UX Design Problems

- Dashboard resembles "WordPress admin -- functional but not visually refined"
- Analytics look like "PowerPoint presentations with rudimentary design"
- Website/funnel builder rated 5-6/10, compared to "Wix from the 2010s"
- Settings "scattered across opposite sides of the screen"
- Contact management feels "squished" and "too much stuff jammed in"
- "Developer-driven rather than user-driven" design
- "Old school early 2000s vibes, weird font pairings, numerous design mistakes"

### #7: Automation Reliability / Platform Bugs

- **171 completely irrelevant emails sent to wrong users** over three days
- Workflows fire incorrectly, duplicate emails sent, wrong contacts receive messages
- Platform updates **break existing automations without warning**
- Website builder save/publish failures lasting **65+ days**
- Instagram planner broken for **30+ days**
- Common user sentiment: "Stop adding features and FIX all the bugs"

### #8: Billing & Cancellation Issues

- Cannot self-cancel trial -- requires emailing support
- Users charged **$497 instead of $97 trial price** with no refund
- 22 BBB complaints in 3 years, 8 specifically billing-related
- Continued charges after documented cancellation attempts
- Cancellation takes up to 36 hours to process
- Phone numbers continue to be charged 14 days after cancellation

## 3. Feature-by-Feature Gap Analysis

### Website Builder

- **No staging environment** -- all changes go live immediately
- No plugin ecosystem
- No global styling system, no custom fonts (Google Fonts only)
- No text animations or advanced design capabilities
- Blog URLs hardcoded to `/post/post-name` -- cannot change slug structure
- Blog pages can't customize headers, footers, side columns
- Page speed: 30-60+ second load times on mobile
- **Users want:** Webflow-level design flexibility, staging environments, SEO tools

### CRM

- Contact records cluttered with scattered settings
- Not suitable for complex sales cycles or large teams
- No consolidated cross-pipeline reporting
- Limited forecasting and activity tracking
- Custom Objects capped at 10 per sub-account
- URLs not clickable in notes/custom fields
- Revenue per staff member not available as default feature
- **Users want:** HubSpot-level reporting, different custom fields per pipeline, cleaner interface

### Ecommerce

- No inventory management, no variant handling at scale
- No digital product support -- only physical goods
- Manual fulfillment only -- no fulfillment workflow
- Cannot pick which products appear in which store (all or nothing)
- Cannot bulk-add products to collections
- Cannot delete orders even after deleting user accounts
- No drop-shipping or print-on-demand integration
- Only Stripe/PayPal/Authorize.net/NMI supported
- **Users want:** Shopify-level ecommerce with inventory, fulfillment, digital products

### Courses & Memberships

- Only 3 themes available for customization
- Content limited to video and image (no audio)
- Everything forced into "courses" framework -- no general membership pages
- Student interface "dated" with limited branding
- Checkout tied to funnel builder, not purpose-built
- No newsletters within membership areas
- **Users want:** Kajabi-level course experience with flexible membership areas

### Social Media Planner

- Analytics limited to post-level vanity metrics
- TikTok posting only via mobile app, personal accounts only
- LinkedIn: pages only, no personal profiles
- No social listening, attribution modeling, or competitor analysis
- Cannot select date ranges to view statistics
- **Users want:** Instagram auto-DM to followers (915 votes), social listening, advanced analytics

### Calendar & Scheduling

- Google Calendar sync issues with multiple Gmail accounts
- Can only sync one calendar per Google account
- OAuth tokens expire after 90 days without clear error messages
- Double-booking reported across multiple accounts
- Calendar widget slow to load on landing pages
- **Users want:** Calendly-level reliability and polish, multi-calendar sync

### Reporting & Analytics

- Basic metrics only -- "operational, not analytical"
- No multi-touch attribution or revenue forecasting
- Cannot combine disparate metrics into single charts
- Timezone issues cause inaccurate data
- No cross-sub-account roll-up reporting
- No campaign ROI dashboards
- **Users want:** True custom report builder, attribution modeling, executive dashboards

### Blog & SEO

- No built-in enforcement for URLs, metadata, schema, or canonicals
- Routine page edits can change URLs, losing search authority
- Schema codes "simply ignored or filtered out"
- No native Open Graph tags or Twitter Cards
- No real-time SEO feedback (no Yoast/RankMath equivalent)
- Sites found with default titles "Home -- Page 1" and no meta description
- Blogs confirmed slow by Ahrefs analysis
- **Users want:** WordPress-level SEO tools, customizable URL structures

### Invoicing & Billing

- No automated recurring invoices -- must manually duplicate
- Cannot dynamically set invoice amounts from opportunity values
- Cannot attach invoices as PDFs to outgoing emails (marked "complete" but broken)
- Item catalog doesn't sync with accounting platforms (Xero/QuickBooks)
- **Users want:** Automated recurring invoices, full accounting integration

### API

- Rate limits: 100 requests/10 seconds, 200K/day
- Non-standard OAuth (refresh token must be updated every access token refresh)
- Need Marketplace app for simple one-off integrations
- V1 API deprecated but V2 requires significant code changes
- No API development/debugging support from GHL
- **Users want:** Standard OAuth, higher limits, simpler integration path

### Affiliate Manager

- Affiliates cannot self-register -- manual add only
- Payouts are fully manual (PayPal/bank transfer)
- $50 minimum payout threshold
- No ability to limit commission duration on subscriptions
- **Users want:** Self-registration, automated payouts, commission duration limits

### Mobile App

- Limited feature set vs desktop
- No funnel editing, workflow management, or detailed reports
- Cannot customize white-label app interface per client
- Updates sometimes break existing features
- v4.0 (2025) improved but still lacks parity
- **Users want:** Desktop feature parity, client-facing customization

### AI Features

- AI Employee: $97/month add-on (total: $194-$594/month)
- Voice AI handles only 70-80% of calls autonomously
- Fails on complex situations requiring empathy or variable pricing
- Content AI throttled at high volume
- Reviews AI described as "introductory features"
- Conversation AI in autopilot may mishandle conversations
- Voice AI cost: $0.06-$0.13/min + AI tokens + phone charges
- **Users want:** More robust AI, transparent pricing, better performance benchmarks

## 4. White-Label & Agency-Specific Pain Points

### White-Label Issues

- Mobile app requires Agency Pro plan + additional fee
- Platform UI "feels dated with early 2000s vibes" -- agencies must present this to clients
- Shared email domain causes branding issues
- Resellers **entirely responsible for customer support** -- GHL won't help end users
- Minimum pricing rule: must charge at least $97/month per sub-account
- Lifetime access sales prohibited without prior approval
- Complex DNS setup for non-standard domain providers

### SaaS Mode Limitations

- Limited to 3 pricing plan tiers
- No business model guidance (positioning, onboarding, retention)
- Conversation AI cannot collect payments during booking flow
- Active operational management required -- "not passive income"

### Multi-Location Management

- Described as "a complete nightmare"
- LocationId is called "an underlying design flaw"
- Forces location-based silos instead of centralized management
- Managing Google Business Profiles, contacts, communications separately per location is impractical

- Users migrating from best-in-class dedicated tools

## 7. What GHL Does Well (Competitor Advantages to Match)

1. **White-label + SaaS resale** -- No competitor offers this at $297-$497/mo
2. **All-in-one consolidation** -- Replaces 10+ tools, saves $400-600/mo
3. **Sub-account architecture** -- Unlimited client accounts from one agency account
4. **Snapshots** -- Clone entire setups across accounts in minutes
5. **Native SMS/Voice** -- Built-in (most competitors need Twilio add-ons)
6. **Unlimited contacts** -- No per-contact pricing
7. **Active development** -- 200+ features shipped in 2025, weekly updates
8. **Workflow automation** -- Powerful multi-channel sequences (when they work)
9. **Community** -- 100K+ members, active Facebook group, YouTube ecosystem

## 9. Top Opportunities for a Competitor (Surgent)

Based on the full analysis, these are the clearest gaps a competitor can exploit:

### Tier 1: Massive, Universal Pain Points

1. **Performance & reliability** -- GHL is slow and buggy. A fast, stable platform wins immediately
2. **Modern UI/UX** -- GHL's interface universally criticized. Clean, intuitive design is a major differentiator
3. **Email deliverability** -- GHL's biggest functional weakness. Solving this is table stakes
4. **Transparent, predictable pricing** -- No hidden usage-based costs that surprise users

### Tier 2: High-Impact Feature Gaps

5. **Better reporting & analytics** -- Multi-touch attribution, cross-account dashboards, campaign ROI
6. **Website builder that actually works** -- Staging environments, global styles, fast page speed, SEO tools
7. **Real ecommerce** -- Inventory, fulfillment, digital products, variants
8. **Multi-location management** -- Centralized control instead of location silos
9. **Superior course/membership platform** -- Kajabi-level without separate subscription

### Tier 3: Differentiation Opportunities

10. **True white-label quality** -- No "GHL clutter," modern UI that agencies are proud to show clients
11. **Global SMS/phone availability** -- GHL's Twilio dependency locks out many regions
12. **Support that resolves issues** -- Consistent, knowledgeable support without $300/mo premium tier
13. **Social media AI** -- Instagram auto-DM (915 votes), social listening, content generation
14. **Helpdesk/ticketing built-in** -- 859 votes, requested for 5+ years, still not built

### The Core Strategic Insight

> GHL's moat is consolidation: agencies stay because switching 10+ tools is painful, even when each individual tool is mediocre. A competitor doesn't need to beat GHL at everything on day one -- it needs to match the consolidation value while **excelling at the 3-4 things GHL does worst**: performance, UI/UX, email deliverability, and pricing transparency. Those four alone would capture the massive frustrated-but-locked-in segment.
