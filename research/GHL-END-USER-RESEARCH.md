# GoHighLevel End-User Research: Who Agencies Sell To

_Research date: March 31, 2026_

---

## Executive Summary

The GHL end user is the **local small business owner** -- dentist, plumber, lawyer, salon owner, gym owner. 36 million of them exist in the US. 71% do marketing themselves and fail. Only 8% use agencies. 52% spend under $1,000/month total on marketing. They've been burned before, trust referrals above all else, and want proof before paying. The SaaS model (GHL white-label) churns 2-4x less than agency services because it gives clients transparency and control at 1/10th the cost.

---

## 1. The End-User Profile

- **Role:** Owner-operator of a local service business
- **Employees:** 1-15 people
- **Revenue:** $200K-$5M/year
- **Marketing budget:** 52% spend under $1,000/month total; 47-49% spend under $500/month
- **Time for marketing:** 56% have ONE HOUR OR LESS per day
- **Tech level:** Low. 44% don't consider digital tech essential. 27% have no website. 55% still work on paper.
- **DIY vs agency:** 71% do ALL marketing themselves. Only 8% use agencies.
- **Fear:** "I've been burned before" -- the #1 emotional barrier
- **Trust:** 82% rely on referrals. Won't trust cold pitches.
- **Behavior:** Maintains DIY consistency for ~20 days, then loses momentum and repeats cycle

## 6. Buying Behavior

### How They Choose

- 82% rely on referrals (nothing else comes close)
- 80% say "proven results" are very important
- Price is the #1 factor
- 74% want a "one-stop shop"
- 38% won't contact you if you have negative reviews

### The Typical Buying Cycle

- Day 1-20: Try DIY marketing, post on social media
- Day 20: Lose momentum, stop, go back to running business
- Day 21-60: Repeat cycle 2-3 times
- Day 60+: Admit they need help
- Day 61-75: Ask friends for referrals, Google "[industry] marketing"
- Day 75-90: Talk to 2-3 options, get pitched
- Day 90: Sign with whoever has best proof + lowest risk
- Day 91-180: Make-or-break. 43% of churn happens in first 90 days

### What Convinces Them

1. Referrals from trusted peers
2. Proof from similar businesses (same industry, specific numbers)
3. Low-risk entry (month-to-month, no contract, low starting price)
4. Transparent reporting (real-time dashboard, not monthly PDF)
5. Speed of results (reviews and leads in first 2 weeks)

### Top Objections

1. "I've been burned before / I don't trust agencies"
2. "It's too expensive"
3. "I can do it myself" (71% believe this)
4. "I don't have time to deal with this"
5. "I doubt your ability to get results"

## 8. Churn Data

### By Service Type (Annual Churn)

| Service                      | Annual Churn |
| ---------------------------- | ------------ |
| Full-service digital agency  | 25%          |
| Content marketing            | 35%          |
| SEO                          | 38%          |
| Email marketing              | 41%          |
| Social media                 | 46%          |
| PPC / Paid ads               | 49%          |
| **SaaS product (GHL model)** | **~13%**     |

### Why They Leave

1. Unmet performance expectations (over-promising → disappointment)
2. Can't see ROI ("I don't know if this is working")
3. Communication breakdown (agency goes silent)
4. Budget cuts (economy tightens)
5. Poor customer service (67% leave for this reason)

### Key Timing

- 43% of all SMB customer losses happen in first 90 days
- Time-to-value must be under 14 days
- Monthly billing churns 2-3x more than annual
- Higher ARPU ($250+) = lower churn (5.1% vs 8.7% for $25-50)

## 12. Strategic Implications for Surgent

### The Market Gap

```
AGENCY MODEL:     $2,500-$15,000/mo  →  Only 8% of SMBs can afford this
GHL SaaS MODEL:   $97-$497/mo        →  Still requires agency as intermediary
THE GAP:          $49-$149/mo         →  Direct to the 36M businesses doing it alone
```

### The Opportunity

- 71% do marketing themselves and fail (20-day consistency cycle)
- 52% spend under $1,000/mo total
- 60% already use AI for marketing
- SMBs using AI are 5.7x more likely to succeed
- "Done-with-you" (AI does heavy lifting, owner feels in control) is the underserved middle

### Who to Target First

1. **Home services** (HVAC, plumbing, roofing, landscaping) -- 800K+ businesses, clear ROI, understand leads = revenue
2. **Dental/Medical** -- 178K+ practices, high patient CLV, need scheduling + reviews
3. **Professional services** (lawyers, insurance, mortgage) -- 400K+ firms, high per-client values

### What They Need (in order of value)

1. Reviews (visible results in days)
2. Missed call text-back (instant lead capture)
3. Booking/scheduling (reduce no-shows)
4. Automated follow-up (speed to lead)
5. Website (online presence)
6. Social media (awareness)
