# Surgent White-Label Product Blueprint: 10 Tools, Standalone or Together

_Research date: March 30, 2026_

---

## The Concept

Build 10 tools. Each one works on its own as a standalone white-label product. But they all share a common data layer (the business profile + contacts), so when you use them together, they're 10x more powerful than separate tools. Resellers can sell one tool, three tools, or all ten -- under their own brand.

Think of it like Zoho (50+ individual apps, or Zoho One for everything) meets HubSpot (individual Hubs or the full platform) -- but fully white-labelable for agencies to resell.

## How Each Tool Works Standalone vs Together

### Tool 1: SITE (Website Builder)

**Standalone value:** SMB gets a fast, modern website with SEO built in. AI generates it from business description. Edit visually or tell AI what to change.

**What it does alone:**

- AI-generated website from business description
- Visual editor with global styles (fonts, colors, spacing)
- Blog with customizable URLs
- SEO auto-configured (meta tags, schema, sitemap, OG tags)
- Mobile-first responsive
- SSL, hosting, CDN included
- Contact form that emails the business owner

**When connected to other tools:**

- - Chat → Chatbot widget appears on the website automatically
- - Cal → Booking widget embeds on services/contact pages
- - Rep → Review widget shows Google/Facebook stars on homepage
- - Mail → Form submissions go into CRM, trigger email sequences
- - Pipe → Website visitor tracking feeds into pipeline lead scoring
- - Social → Blog posts auto-generate social media content

**Who buys standalone:** Web design agencies, freelancers selling websites. Charge clients $500-$3,000 setup + $49-$149/mo hosting.

### Tool 10: FLOW (Automation Builder)

**Standalone value:** Visual builder to connect triggers and actions across all tools. If THIS happens, do THAT.

**What it does alone (needs at least 1 other tool):**

- Visual drag-and-drop workflow builder
- Pre-built templates per industry (dental, HVAC, real estate, restaurant, gym)
- Triggers from any tool (new lead, missed call, new review, appointment booked, payment received, etc.)
- Actions to any tool (send email, send SMS, create contact, update pipeline, request review, etc.)
- Conditions and branching (if/then logic)
- Wait steps (delay 1 hour, wait until Monday, etc.)
- Goal completion tracking

**Pre-built templates examples:**

- "New Lead Speed-to-Follow-Up" (lead comes in → immediate SMS → email at 1h → call task at 4h)
- "No-Show Recovery" (missed appointment → SMS at 15min → email at 1h → reschedule link at 24h)
- "Post-Service Review Request" (appointment completed → thank you SMS → review request at 2h → follow-up at 48h)
- "Birthday Campaign" (birthday field matches → morning email with offer → SMS reminder)
- "Re-Engagement" (no activity 90 days → "we miss you" email → offer → SMS)

**When connected to other tools:**

- Flow is the GLUE. It connects every tool to every other tool
- Without Flow, tools work in isolation
- With Flow, the whole platform becomes an automation engine

**Who buys standalone:** Nobody buys Flow alone. It's the upsell that makes bundles sticky. "You're using Site + Mail? Add Flow and your contact form submissions automatically trigger email sequences." THIS is what increases ARPU and reduces churn.

### The Key Insight: Land and Expand

Research from HubSpot, Zoho, Freshworks, Atlassian all confirms:

1. **Land** with ONE tool solving ONE pain point (Rep is the easiest entry: "Get more 5-star reviews")

**Data backs this up:**

- Single-product retention after 2 years: only **30%**
- Bundled products: dramatically higher retention
- Effective bundles increase ARPU by **15-40%**
- Bundles perceived as **20-25% more valuable** than sum of parts

## The Shared Foundation (What Makes Tools Better Together)

### Business Profile (The Core)

Every tool reads from and writes to one shared business profile:

```
Business Profile
├── Name, description, industry
├── Services (name, description, price, duration)
├── Hours of operation
├── Location(s)
├── Brand voice / tone
├── Target audience
├── Team members
└── Assets (logo, photos, brand colors)
```

- Site uses this to generate the website
- Social uses this to generate relevant content
- Chat uses this to answer questions about services/pricing
- Voice uses this to handle phone inquiries
- Cal uses this to show services and availability
- Mail uses this to personalize email sequences
- Rep uses this to know when to request reviews

**This is the moat.** When you use 3+ tools, they all know the business. Change your pricing in the business profile, and the website updates, the chatbot answers correctly, the voice agent quotes accurately, the booking page reflects it, and the email templates include it. In GHL, you update this in 7 different places.

### Contact Database (Shared CRM)

Every tool shares one contact record:

```
Contact: Sarah Martinez
├── Basic info (name, email, phone)
├── Source: Instagram Ad → Chat conversation → Booked appointment
├── Timeline:
│   ├── Mar 28: Clicked Instagram ad (Social)
│   ├── Mar 28: Chatted about Invisalign pricing (Chat)
│   ├── Mar 28: Booked consultation for Apr 2 (Cal)
│   ├── Mar 28: Confirmation email sent (Mail)
│   ├── Mar 29: Reminder SMS sent (Mail)
│   ├── Apr 2: Attended appointment (Cal)
│   ├── Apr 2: Invoice sent, $3,500 (Pay)
│   ├── Apr 2: Review request sent (Rep)
│   ├── Apr 3: Left 5-star Google review (Rep)
│   └── Apr 3: Review shared on Instagram (Social)
├── Pipeline: "Won - $3,500"

```

**Every tool writes to this timeline.** The business owner sees the complete customer journey in one place. No switching between apps. No "which tool has this person's info?"

### AI Engine (Shared Intelligence)

One AI layer powers all tools:

- **Same business knowledge** across all tools (services, pricing, FAQ, tone)
- **Multi-model**: Uses Claude for complex conversations, faster models for simple tasks, specialized models for voice
- **Learning**: Chatbot conversations improve voice agent responses. Email engagement data improves social content. Everything feeds back
- **Content generation**: One engine generates website copy, social posts, email sequences, chatbot responses, review replies -- all in the same brand voice

### What the Reseller Controls

Based on the 120+ touchpoint research, here's what a reseller must be able to brand:

**Visual (5 minutes to set up):**

- Logo (all variations: full, icon, light, dark)
- Colors (primary, secondary, accent -- CSS variables propagate everywhere)
- Fonts (heading + body)
- Favicon + Apple Touch Icon
- Login page (logo, background, colors)

**Domains (15 minutes + DNS propagation):**

- App domain: `app.theiragency.com`
- Client sites domain: `sites.theiragency.com`
- Email sending domain: `mail.theiragency.com`
- Calendar/booking links: `book.theiragency.com`
- API/tracking links: `go.theiragency.com`

**Communications:**

- Email "From" name and address
- SMS sender ID (where supported)
- Push notification branding
- Invoice header/footer
- Auto-configured SPF/DKIM/DMARC

**Legal:**

- Terms of Service URL (their own)
- Privacy Policy URL (their own)
- Cookie consent text

**Mobile App (if applicable):**

- App name in store
- App icon
- Splash screen
- Store listing description

### Where Brands Typically Leak (And Surgent Must NOT)

Research identified 25 common leak points. The worst offenders:

| Leak Point               | How It Happens                          | Surgent Fix                        |
| ------------------------ | --------------------------------------- | ---------------------------------- |
| Return-Path email header | Shows platform domain in email metadata | Custom Return-Path per reseller    |
| Message-ID email header  | Contains @platform.com                  | Generate with reseller domain      |
| Tracking/shortened links | go.platformname.com/xxx                 | Reseller's branded short domain    |
| OAuth screens            | "Authorize PlatformName to access..."   | Reseller-branded OAuth             |
| Error pages              | 404/500 pages show platform name        | Reseller-branded error pages       |
| Help documentation       | help.platformname.com                   | Embeddable docs on reseller domain |
| HTML source code         | Meta generator tags, CSS class prefixes | Strip all platform references      |
| HTTP headers             | X-Powered-By, Server headers            | Remove or replace                  |
| Calendar invite sender   | "via PlatformName" in calendar apps     | Clean calendar invites             |
| App store developer name | "Built by PlatformName Inc"             | Reseller's developer account       |

**The goal: a technical person inspecting the product should NOT be able to identify it as Surgent.** This is what "100% white-label" actually means, and what competitors fail at.

## The Billing Architecture

### Money Flow

```
End Client (SMB) ──$297/mo──> Reseller (Agency) ──$179/mo──> Surgent
                                    │
                              Keeps $118/mo
                              (40% margin)

### How It Works Technically

**Stripe Connect (Custom Accounts)**

1. Reseller connects their Stripe account during setup
2. Surgent creates a Custom Connected Account (fully white-labeled -- client never sees "Surgent" or "Stripe")
3. When a reseller's client pays, the money goes to the reseller's Stripe
4. Surgent automatically collects its platform fee from the reseller via application fee or separate subscription

**Feature Gating / Entitlements**

The reseller creates pricing plans for their clients:

```

## Architecture (How to Build This)

### Start Monolith, Design Modular

Research confirms: "For 99% of early-stage products, a properly architected monolith is optimal." Don't start with microservices. Start with:

**One codebase, DDD bounded contexts:**

```
surgent/
├── core/
│   ├── business-profile/     (shared business data)
│   ├── contacts/             (shared contact database)
│   ├── auth/                 (shared authentication + tenancy)
│   ├── billing/              (Stripe Connect, entitlements, feature gating)
│   ├── ai/                   (shared AI engine, multi-model)
│   ├── theming/              (branding, white-label config)
│   └── analytics/            (shared event tracking)
├── tools/
│   ├── site/                 (website builder)
│   ├── social/               (social media management)
│   ├── mail/                 (email + SMS)
│   ├── chat/                 (AI chatbot)
│   ├── voice/                (AI voice agent)
│   ├── cal/                  (booking + scheduling)
│   ├── pipe/                 (CRM + pipeline)
│   ├── rep/                  (reputation management)
│   ├── pay/                  (invoicing + payments)
│   └── flow/                 (automation builder)
└── platform/
    ├── agency-dashboard/     (reseller management UI)
    ├── client-dashboard/     (end-user UI, shows only enabled tools)
    ├── saas-configurator/    (plan builder, feature gating)
    └── snapshot-manager/     (template system)
```

### Key Technical Decisions

**Tenant isolation:**

- Every request carries `tenant_id` (the client account)
- Database: PostgreSQL with Row-Level Security (RLS)
- Each reseller = an organization with multiple client tenants
- Each client tenant has its own data, branding config, and enabled tools

**Feature gating:**

- Entitlements stored per tenant, connected to billing plan
- Each tool checks entitlements before rendering in UI or processing requests
- Disabled tools are invisible (not grayed out) in the client UI

**Event bus:**

- Tools communicate via events, not direct calls
- "appointment.completed" event → Mail tool sends follow-up, Rep tool sends review request, Pipe tool updates stage
- This is what makes Flow (automation builder) possible -- it subscribes to ALL events

**Theming:**

- Branding config stored as JSON per organization (reseller)
- CSS variables generated from config, applied at runtime
- Custom domain routing via reverse proxy (Caddy/Nginx)
- Each reseller's domain resolves to the same app, different theme

**AI layer:**

- One AI service that accepts context (business profile + tool-specific data)
- Multi-model routing: Claude for complex reasoning, Haiku for fast tasks, Whisper for voice transcription, etc.
- Shared vector store per tenant for business knowledge (FAQ, services, policies)

Based on research, the order to build and launch:

```
PHASE 1 (MVP - First 3 tools + Foundation):
├── Foundation: Business Profile, Contacts, Auth, Billing, Theming
├── Tool: Rep (reputation management) -- EASIEST SELL, foot-in-door
├── Tool: Cal (booking) -- EVERY service business needs this
├── Tool: Site (website) -- HIGH PERCEIVED VALUE
├── Flow: Basic (connect these 3 tools)
└── White-label: Full branding + agency dashboard

    Strategy: "Get more reviews, let clients book online, look professional"
    Reseller charges: $99-$199/mo per client
    Surgent charges reseller: $297/mo platform fee (unlimited clients)

PHASE 2 (Growth - Add AI + Communication):
├── Tool: Chat (AI chatbot)
├── Tool: Voice (AI voice agent)
├── Tool: Mail (email + SMS)
└── Flow: Expanded (all 6 tools connected)

    Strategy: "Your business runs on autopilot 24/7"
    Reseller charges: $297-$497/mo per client
    Surgent charges: $297-$497/mo platform fee

PHASE 3 (Full Suite):
├── Tool: Social (social media)
├── Tool: Pipe (CRM + pipeline)
├── Tool: Pay (invoicing)
├── Flow: Full automation builder with templates
├── Snapshot marketplace
└── Mobile app (white-label PWA or native)

    Strategy: "One platform replaces everything"
    Reseller charges: $297-$997/mo per client
    Surgent charges: $497/mo platform fee
```

### Why This Order?

1. **Rep first** -- Research says it's "#1 service for white-label resellers." Low friction. Measurable results. Every business wants more reviews. This is the foot-in-the-door
2. **Cal second** -- Every service business needs booking. Calendly/Acuity are NOT white-label. Clear gap
3. **Site third** -- High perceived value ($500-$3K websites), and it becomes the HOME for Rep (review widget) and Cal (booking widget)
4. **Chat + Voice in Phase 2** -- These are the high-margin AI products ($500-$2,000/mo). By Phase 2 you have the foundation + clients
5. **Social + Pipe + Pay in Phase 3** -- These complete the suite but aren't the initial hook
